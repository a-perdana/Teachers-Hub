This page explains the KPI Management section of your Admin menu — what KPIs are, how the cycle works, and what to do on each of the two pages. KPI = Key Performance Indicator: a small list of numbers that measure how your teaching is going this period.
For: Subject Teachers & Subject Leaders 2 pages: Self Assessment · Self Results Runs on a window (opens / closes) Anchored to Cambridge Teacher Standards 2023
KPI is one of four growth systems — KPI, AI Framework, Appraisal, Competency. KPI is the numbers one. It never decides your appraisal grade, and your appraisal never changes your KPI. They stay separate by design.

The KPI Management section in the Admin menu has 2 pages. KPI stands for Key Performance Indicator — a short list of measurable goals your school sets for the year (for example: "average class result ≥ 80%", or "pacing on track"). Once per period, you rate yourself against each one. This page explains the whole idea in plain English. If a word looks new, Section 6 — Glossary explains it.

A short promise
You do not need to memorise every KPI today. The section runs the same simple cycle every period: Eduversal opens a window → you self-rate → your school reviews → you read the result. Once you know the cycle, both pages explain themselves. Come back here if you get stuck.
🎯
What is a KPI?
A clear, measurable goal. Each KPI has a name, a weight (how much it counts, as a %), a target (the bar to hit), and a Cambridge tag (which teaching standard it links to). All your KPI weights add up to 100%.
👥
Who uses this section?
Every Subject Teacher and Subject Leader who has KPI enabled at their school. You do the self-rating; your school principal or academic coordinator (over on Academic Hub) reviews it and agrees the final score.
One important separation. KPI is the numbers system. It never feeds your Appraisal grade, and your Appraisal never changes your KPI score. The two are deliberately kept apart so each stays honest. See Section 6 for the words.

Two pages. Here is exactly what each one is for, when to use it, and what you will see.

⭐
KPI Self Assessment
Admin · KPI Management · /teacher-self-assessment
Open
What it is
A form where you rate your own performance against each KPI for the open period, on a 1–5 star scale, with a short written note as evidence.
When to use it
When a KPI period is open. You will usually be told (by your school or by email). If no period is open, the page tells you so.
What you see
  • The list of KPIs your school set, each with its weight and target.
  • A star rating to set per KPI.
  • A note box to explain your rating.
  • A save button — you can return and edit until you submit.
Good to know
Be honest. Self-rating is the start of the conversation, not the final word. Your school sees your self-rating next to their own view.
📈
KPI Self Results
Admin · KPI Management · /teacher-kpi-results
Open
What it is
A read-only view of your finalised KPI result for a period — the agreed score per KPI plus any development goals your school added.
When to use it
After your school finishes reviewing. Until then it may say the result is not ready yet.
What you see
  • Your score per KPI and the weighted total.
  • Development goals — small next steps to grow.
  • A history of past periods, if any.
Good to know
This page is for reading, not editing. If a number looks wrong, talk to your school leader — they own the final score.

A soft list, not a test. These keep your KPI period stress-free.

1
Read your KPIs at the start of the year
Open KPI Self Assessment once early. Knowing your targets from day one makes them easier to hit.
2
Keep small notes during the term
When something goes well in class, jot it down. At self-assessment time, those notes become your evidence.
3
Self-rate honestly
A fair self-rating builds trust. Over-rating or under-rating both make the review conversation harder.
4
Read your result and act on the goals
After the period, open KPI Self Results. The development goals are the most useful part — they point to your next growth step.
Tip: KPI work links naturally to your Competency Framework growth and your Teacher Appraisal. The same good teaching shows up in all three — but each is scored separately.

Short, plain meanings for the terms that show up in the KPI pages.

KPIKey Performance Indicator
One measurable goal your school sets for the year. Example: "average class result ≥ 80%". You have a small set of them.
Weighthow much it counts
A percentage showing how important a KPI is. All your KPI weights add up to 100%. A KPI with a higher weight moves your total score more.
Targetthe bar to hit
The number you are aiming for on a KPI — for example 80%, or 4.0 stars. Some KPIs have a mid-year and an end-of-year target.
Window / Periodwhen KPI is open
The time when you can fill in your self-assessment. Eduversal opens and closes it. Outside the window, the pages are read-only or quiet.
Self-Assessmentyour own rating
When you rate yourself first, before your school reviews. It starts the conversation about your performance.
Evidenceyour short note
A sentence or two explaining why you gave a rating. It helps your reviewer understand your view.
CTSCambridge Teacher Standards 2023
The Cambridge document our KPIs link to. A tag like CTS 4.1 means standard 4.1. Click the chip to read the source text.
Development goalyour next step
A small, named action your school suggests to help you grow, shown on your KPI Self Results page.
