📋 How to Complete This Assessment ▼
1
Check your teacher information

Verify your name, subject, grade, and department in the sidebar panel. These details will be included in your submission.

2
Expand each KPI card and enter your actual performance

Click a KPI card to open it. Enter your actual performance value (e.g. 92% for engagement rate). The target and benchmark are shown at the top of each card for reference.

3
Select your self-rating (1–5 stars)

Choose a rating that honestly reflects your performance: 1 = Below Expectations, 2 = Needs Improvement, 3 = Meets Expectations, 4 = Exceeds Expectations, 5 = Outstanding.

4
Add evidence and complete reflections

Click "Add Evidence" and document specific examples (lesson plans, assessment data, student work). Then fill in the four reflection fields: Strengths, Challenges, Improvement Actions, and Support Needed.

5
Save draft, then submit when ready

Use "Save Draft" at any time to avoid losing progress. Once all KPIs are complete, click "Submit Assessment". You can re-submit before the deadline if you need to make changes.

💡 Tips for Effective Self-Assessment ▼
✔️Be honest and realistic — an accurate self-assessment helps your coordinator support you better than an inflated one.
✔️Use specific examples and data — reference lesson plans, test scores, or student work to support your ratings.
✔️Focus on growth, not perfection — identifying development areas is a strength, not a weakness.
✔️Identify concrete improvement actions — vague intentions are less useful than specific, time-bound steps.
✔️Don't be afraid to ask for support — the Support Needed field exists so your school can respond to your needs.
Preparing for your evaluation meeting
📄Re-read your self-assessment before meeting your evaluator and be ready to discuss your ratings and evidence.
📁Bring physical or digital copies of key evidence: lesson plans, student work, assessment data, certificates.
🙋Prepare questions about available professional development and support resources.
🤝Be open to feedback — the goal is professional growth, not judgement.
