This page explains the Competency section of your Admin menu. The Competency Framework is a read-only map of the teaching skills Eduversal wants every teacher to grow — grouped into 7 domains, with 28 skills, each describable at 4 stages of growth.
For: all teaching staff 7 domains × 28 competencies 4 growth stages per skill Cambridge · ES · AICF anchored
The Competency Framework is the map. You actually grow your skills over in My Hub — Learning Path, My Portfolio, My Certificates. The framework never grades you; it shows you what "good" looks like at each stage.

The Competency section in the Admin menu holds the Competency Framework page. A competency is a specific teaching skill — for example, "use questioning to check understanding". The framework lists them all, grouped into 7 domains, and describes what each one looks like at 4 stages of growth. This page explains it in plain English. If a word looks new, Section 5 — Glossary explains it.

A short promise
The framework is a map, not a test. You read it to understand what skilled teaching looks like and to pick where to grow next. There is no score and no deadline here — your actual growth happens in My Hub, at your own pace. Come back here if you get stuck.
🗺️
What is it?
A clear map of the teaching skills we value — 7 domains, 28 competencies. Each skill is described at 4 stages (from Awareness to Lead) so you can see your next step.
👥
Who uses it?
All teaching staff. It is open to read any time. It also links to your KPI and Appraisal — the same good teaching shows up everywhere — but the framework itself just describes; it does not score you.
Where the growth happens. The Competency Framework page is the map. To actually build a skill, head to My Hub: Learning Path (read the lesson), My Portfolio (submit evidence), My Certificates (earn a domain certificate).

The 28 skills are grouped into 7 domains. Each skill can be described at 4 growth stages. You read the stage that matches you and aim for the next one.

The 7 domains

SMC Subject Mastery & Cambridge LCP Learner-Centred Pedagogy AFL Assessment for Learning ICP Innovative Classroom Practice PIE Professional Identity & Ethics CCE Collaboration & Community AID AI & Digital Literacy

The 4 stages

Stage 1
Awareness
You know the skill exists and why it matters.
Stage 2
Practitioner
You can do it with some help or guidance.
Stage 3
Advanced
You do it well on your own, consistently.
Stage 4
Lead
You can teach or mentor others to do it.
Earn a certificate per domain. When all the skills in a domain reach the top stage (verified through your portfolio evidence), you earn a domain certificate. You collect them in My Certificates.

What it is
A read-only browser of the Teachers competency framework — 7 domains × 28 skills, each with descriptions at 4 stages. Plus a small progress dashboard showing where you are.
When to use it
Any time. Browse it early in the year to understand the skills, and return when choosing your next growth focus.
What you see
  • The 7 domains, each opening to its skills.
  • Each skill described at 4 stages.
  • Chips linking to the source standards — CTS, ES, AICF. Click any to read the source.
  • A progress overview of your earned stages.
Good to know
This page describes — it does not score you. To move a skill up a stage, use Learning Path + My Portfolio in My Hub.
Tip: The mor CTS, cyan ES, and orange AICF chips throughout Teachers Hub all link to the source frameworks. Click one any time you want the exact wording behind a skill.

Short, plain meanings for the terms in the Competency Framework.

Competencyalso: skill
A specific teaching skill we expect you to grow. Example: "use questioning to check understanding". Each one has 4 stages.
Domainalso: area
A group of related skills. There are 7: SMC, LCP, AFL, ICP, PIE, CCE, AID. A certificate is earned per domain.
Stage / Level4 steps of growth
Awareness → Practitioner → Advanced → Lead. Each describes how confidently you can use a skill.
Evidencein My Portfolio
A short written description (+ optional file) that proves you have practised a skill at a stage. A reviewer reads it before granting the stage.
CTSCambridge Teacher Standards 2023
The Cambridge document the framework anchors to. A tag like CTS 4.1 means standard 4.1. Click the mor chip to read the source.
ESEduversal Academic Standards
Our network policy manual. A tag like ES 7.3 means madde 7.3. Click the cyan chip to read it.
AICFAI Competency Framework
Eduversal's AI literacy framework. It grounds the AID (AI & Digital Literacy) domain. Click the orange chip to read the source.
Certificateper domain
Earned when all skills in a domain reach the top stage. Collected in My Certificates.
