# Slide Deck Outline — The Three Rating Systems

> **How to use:** one `## Slide N` = one slide. **Bullets** = on-screen text (keep them short — the speaker says the rest). **Speaker note** = what the facilitator says. **Visual** = a picture / layout suggestion. ESL/EAL rule: short bullets, gloss any term on the slide.

---

## Slide 1 — Title
- **The Three Rating Systems**
- KPI · Appraisal · Competency
- Eduversal Professional Development

**Speaker note:** "Today we clear up the question almost everyone asks: how are these three different?"
**Visual:** Eduversal mor/cyan title slide; three simple icons.

## Slide 2 — The problem
- People mix up three systems.
- They are **separate on purpose**.
- Each does a **different job**.

**Speaker note:** Ask: "Has anyone been unsure which system a meeting was about?" Let a few answer.
**Visual:** three boxes that look similar — a question mark over them.

## Slide 3 — The one-sentence version
- **KPI** = your results this year.
- **Appraisal** = how you teach (one observation).
- **Competency** = your long-term growth.

**Speaker note:** "If you remember one slide, remember this one."
**Visual:** three columns, one line each.

## Slide 4 — KPI
- **K**ey **P**erformance **I**ndicator
- 15 targets · numbers + weights · total 100%
- Owned by **your school** · checked mid-year + year-end

**Speaker note:** "Results this year. Your school sets the weights and targets."
**Visual:** a simple weighted list.

## Slide 5 — Appraisal
- How you teach · scored **1–4** → letter **A–F**
- 4 parts: F1 (20%) · **F2 = 50%** · F3 (20%) · F4 (info only)
- Owned by the **Eduversal network** (same for all schools)

**Speaker note:** "Half your grade is one observed lesson — F2. We will train this carefully."
**Visual:** a pie chart: F2 half, the rest split.

## Slide 6 — Competency
- Your **long-term growth map**
- 4 levels: Awareness → Practitioner → Advanced → Lead
- You **earn** levels with **evidence** — no single score

**Speaker note:** "This one is yours. You move up over years, with evidence."
**Visual:** a 4-rung ladder.

## Slide 7 — Side by side
- (Show the comparison table: who owns it / scale / does it score me / clock)

**Speaker note:** Walk the table row by row. Pause on "does it score me?".
**Visual:** the 3-column comparison table from the one-pager.

## Slide 8 — One backbone
- All three sit on **Cambridge Teacher Standards 2023**.
- Different lenses · same picture of good teaching.

**Speaker note:** "They are not three random checklists. They look at the same Cambridge standards from three angles."
**Visual:** the ASCII tree from the one-pager, drawn cleanly: Cambridge at top → 3 systems below.

## Slide 9 — Different clocks
- KPI = **this year**
- Appraisal = a **yearly snapshot** of practice
- Competency = a **multi-year journey**

**Speaker note:** "They run on different clocks. That is why you cannot swap one for another."
**Visual:** three timelines of different lengths.

## Slide 10 — Common questions
- A on Appraisal ≠ automatic "Lead" on Competency
- KPI does not change your Appraisal letter
- A school can use any one alone

**Speaker note:** Take real questions here. This is where confusion clears.
**Visual:** Q&A layout.

## Slide 11 — Close
- Keep the one-pager.
- Three systems · three jobs · one Cambridge backbone.
- Next sessions go deeper into each.

**Speaker note:** "Bring the one-pager to every session that follows."
**Visual:** the three-column summary again + "keep this page".
