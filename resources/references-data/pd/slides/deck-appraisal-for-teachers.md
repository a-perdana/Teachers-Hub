# Slide Deck Outline — Understanding Your Appraisal

> One `## Slide N` = one slide. Bullets = on-screen. Speaker note = what you say. ESL/EAL: short bullets, gloss terms.

## Slide 1 — Title
- **Understanding Your Appraisal**
- Eduversal Teacher Appraisal v2.1
- For teachers

**Speaker note:** "By the end, appraisal will feel clear, not scary."

## Slide 2 — The question you brought
- Write your one worry or question about appraisal.

**Speaker note:** Collect on sticky notes / chat. Return to them at the end.
**Visual:** a parking-lot board.

## Slide 3 — What appraisal is
- A once-a-year look at **how you teach**.
- Goal: **growth**, with clear feedback.

**Speaker note:** "Not a trap. A tool."

## Slide 4 — The four parts
- F1 Administration — 20%
- **F2 Class Observation — 50%**
- F3 Work Inspection — 20%
- F4 Student Survey — 10% (**info only**)

**Speaker note:** "Half is one observed lesson. The student survey does NOT change your grade."
**Visual:** pie chart.

## Slide 5 — F2 has four areas
- Lesson Planning
- Classroom Management
- Instructional Process
- Assessment
- (45 small points in total)

**Speaker note:** "These are the four things watched in the lesson."

## Slide 6 — Scored against a rubric
- A **rubric** = a written guide.
- It describes what 1, 2, 3, 4 look like.
- You can **read it before** the observation. Nothing is secret.

**Speaker note:** "The rubric is public. Read it. Plan with it."

## Slide 7 — Read the rubric: 3 vs 4 (activity)
- Level 3: "minor pacing gaps… do not undermine coherence"
- Level 4: "transitions between phases **explicitly planned**"
- Find the one phrase that separates them.

**Speaker note:** Run the pair activity here.
**Visual:** the two rubric levels side by side; the key phrase highlighted.

## Slide 8 — The difference is doable
- "4" is not "be a genius".
- "4" is a **specific, plannable thing**.
- You can do it tomorrow.

**Speaker note:** "Plan your transitions — that is the difference. Specific and doable."

## Slide 9 — Scores → grade
- Each point: 1–4 (Unsatisfactory → Distinguished)
- Combine → letter band **A–F**
- A = Excellent · F = Unsatisfactory

**Speaker note:** Keep this brief — the point is the rubric, not the maths.

## Slide 10 — The feedback: Glow / Grow / Go
- **Glow** — one strength
- **Grow** — one area to grow
- **Go** — one next step, with a date

**Speaker note:** "After the observation, you get this. One of each — not a long list."

## Slide 11 — One backbone
- Every item → a **Cambridge Teacher Standard**
- e.g. planning a clear lesson = **CTS 3.2**
- Same standard for every Eduversal teacher.

**Speaker note:** "Not one school's opinion — the Cambridge standard."

## Slide 12 — Close
- The rubric is public — read it.
- The goal is growth.
- Bring your parked questions back.

**Speaker note:** Answer the parked questions. Exit ticket.
