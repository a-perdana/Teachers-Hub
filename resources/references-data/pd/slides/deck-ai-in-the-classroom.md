# Slide Deck Outline — AI in the Classroom

> One `## Slide N` = one slide. Bullets = on-screen. Speaker note = what you say. ESL/EAL: short bullets, gloss terms.

## Slide 1 — Title
- **AI in the Classroom**
- Use it well · stay safe · hold the line
- For teachers

**Speaker note:** "Practical AI for your own work — not student cheating, which is a separate policy."

## Slide 2 — Two lines moved in 2026
- **Cambridge:** undeclared AI in coursework can void a qualification.
- **Indonesia:** student data in free AI tools is now regulated (UU PDP).

**Speaker note:** "The ground changed. Let us be clear and safe."

## Slide 3 — AI is a tool
- Not a colleague. Not a teacher.
- Fast at: drafting, summarising, restructuring.
- You keep: judgement, planning, feedback.

**Speaker note:** "You stay accountable for every decision."

## Slide 4 — Question 1
- **Does this need my judgement, or is it pattern-shaped drafting?**
- Drafting a lesson skeleton = OK.
- Grading work that counts = NOT OK.

**Speaker note:** Ask the room for examples of each.

## Slide 5 — Question 2
- **Would I be comfortable if a parent, a Cambridge moderator, or my principal saw this prompt?**
- If not → don't send it.

**Speaker note:** "These two questions decide almost everything."

## Slide 6 — The red line: student data
- **Never** put a student's name, photo, or record in a public AI tool.
- Use **anonymous** descriptions instead.

**Speaker note:** Show BAD vs GOOD.
**Visual:**
- BAD: "report for Andi Pratama, Year 8, 65%, trouble focusing"
- GOOD: "report for a Year 8 student, lower end of the class, struggles with attention"

## Slide 7 — Why it matters
- Indonesia's **UU PDP** (data law) enforceable since Oct 2024.
- The school is responsible for what you type.
- Use only **school-approved** tools.

**Speaker note:** "Ask your Academic Coordinator which tools are approved."

## Slide 8 — Quick win (activity)
- Try ONE with **anonymous context only**:
- A) Lesson-plan skeleton (10 min, not 30)
- B) Differentiation in 3 versions (access / on-level / stretch)

**Speaker note:** Demo first, then they try. Always review the output.

## Slide 9 — You stay accountable
- AI gives a draft.
- You give judgement.
- **Always review and edit.**
- Spend saved time on what only you can do.

**Speaker note:** "The skeleton saves 20 minutes — use them to plan for YOUR students."

## Slide 10 — Where it fits
- New competency domain: **AID — AI & Digital Literacy**
- "Awareness" is an honest starting level.

**Speaker note:** "You do not need to be an expert. Start with one safe quick win."

## Slide 11 — Close
- One safe task you will try.
- One red line you will remember.
- Coursework AI questions → ask your Cambridge Coordinator.

**Speaker note:** Exit ticket. "Never 'just try it' for coursework."
