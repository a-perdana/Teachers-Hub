# Slide Deck Outline — Understanding Your KPI

> One `## Slide N` = one slide. Bullets = on-screen. Speaker note = what you say. ESL/EAL: short bullets, gloss terms.

## Slide 1 — Title
- **Understanding Your KPI**
- 15 targets · your year plan
- For teachers

**Speaker note:** "By the end, KPI is a plan you own — not 15 numbers to chase."

## Slide 2 — One word
- One word: how does KPI feel right now?

**Speaker note:** Quick round. Read the room's mood.

## Slide 3 — What KPI is
- **K**ey **P**erformance **I**ndicator
- One thing your school measures this year.
- 15 of them · weights add to 100%.

**Speaker note:** "Results this year. Not how you teach — that is appraisal."

## Slide 4 — What a KPI contains
- **Aspect** — what it is about
- **Indicator** — how it is measured
- **Target** — the number to reach
- **Weight** — how much it counts

**Speaker note:** Walk one example end to end.

## Slide 5 — The 15 (high-weight first)
- 10%: Academic Performance · Engagement · Progress Monitoring · Classroom Management · Inclusive Teaching
- 5%: the other ten

**Speaker note:** "The five at 10% are your school's top priorities."
**Visual:** the 15 listed, grouped by weight.

## Slide 6 — Glossary
- **PLC** = your subject team's planning + learning time
- **CLIL** = teaching subject + English together
- **Walkthrough** = short support visit (not appraisal)

**Speaker note:** Gloss these before they appear in the list.

## Slide 7 — Weights = priorities
- The weight is a **message**.
- It tells you what to focus on first.
- Equal weights = no message.

**Speaker note:** "Read the weights. They are your school's priority order."

## Slide 8 — Two checkpoints
- **Mid-year** target — an early signal
- **End-of-year** target — the goal
- Mid-year is not a grade.

**Speaker note:** "The mid-year point gives you time to adjust."

## Slide 9 — Turn KPI into action (activity)
- Pick ONE KPI for your class.
- Write ONE action + a date.
- Rule: small · dated · in your control.

**Speaker note:** Run the activity. "Make all students get 90%" is not in your control; "weekly retrieval quiz" is.

## Slide 10 — One backbone
- Each KPI → a **Cambridge Teacher Standard**
- e.g. Progress Monitoring = **CTS 3.7**

**Speaker note:** "Your targets sit on the same standards as appraisal and competency."

## Slide 11 — Close
- Read the weights.
- Pick a few · act small · in your control.
- You cannot do all 15 at once — and you should not try.

**Speaker note:** Collect action slips. Exit ticket.
