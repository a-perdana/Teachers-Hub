# How KPI Works — for Teachers

**KPI** stands for **Key Performance Indicator**. A KPI is one thing your school measures to see if your work is on track this year. This page explains the whole system in one read.

> **In one sentence:** your school sets **15 targets** for the year, each with a number and a weight (how much it counts), and you and your leader check your progress at the half-year point and again at the end of the year.

---

## What a KPI is made of

Each of your 15 KPIs has four parts:

- **An aspect** — what it is about (for example, "Classroom Engagement").
- **An indicator** — how it is measured.
- **A target** — the number to reach (for example, "≥90%").
- **A weight** — how much it counts. All weights add up to **100%**.

*Source: 15 KPIs with weights and targets — `teacher-kpi-v1.json`.*

## The 15 things measured (and their weight)

| KPI | Weight |
|---|---|
| Student Academic Performance | 10% |
| Classroom Engagement | 10% |
| Individual Student Progress Monitoring | 10% |
| Classroom Management | 10% |
| Inclusive Teaching Practices | 10% |
| Student Attendance | 5% |
| Professional Development | 5% |
| Student Behavior Management | 5% |
| Support for Special Education Students | 5% |
| Student Satisfaction | 5% |
| Student Wellbeing & Social Development | 5% |
| Lesson Planning & Preparation Discipline | 5% |
| Engagement with Formative Walkthroughs | 5% |
| PLC Monday Commitment | 5% |
| Bilingual Instruction Compliance (CLIL) | 5% |

> **A note on the words:**
> - **PLC** = Professional Learning Community — the time your subject team meets to plan and learn together.
> - **CLIL** = Content and Language Integrated Learning — teaching your subject *and* English at the same time.
> - **Formative walkthrough** = a short, informal classroom visit to support your teaching (not a formal appraisal).

*Source: `teacher-kpi-v1.json` → kpis[].aspect + .weight.*

---

## The targets have two checkpoints

Each KPI has a **half-year** target and a **full-year** target. For example, Student Attendance is **93% by mid-year** and **95% by the end of the year**. The mid-year check is not a grade — it is an early signal, so you have time to act.

---

## The cycle, in three steps

1. **Set** — at the start of the year, you and your leader look at the 15 KPIs and your targets. Your school sets the weights.
2. **Check (mid-year)** — you review progress together. Where are you on track? Where do you need support?
3. **Review (end of year)** — you look at the full-year results and plan next year.

---

## What this means for you

- **The weights are a message.** They tell you what your school cares about most this year. If Academic Performance is 10% and Attendance is 5%, that is your school's priority order — not a judgement on you.
- **It is about results, not personality.** KPI measures outcomes this year. It does not look at *how* you teach in detail — that is Appraisal.
- **Every KPI links to a Cambridge standard.** For example, Progress Monitoring links to CTS 3.7 (giving feedback that moves learning forward). So your targets are not random — they sit on the same standards as everything else.
- **KPI is one of three systems.** It is *not* your Appraisal grade (how you teach) or your Competency level (long-term growth). See *The Three Rating Systems at a Glance*.
