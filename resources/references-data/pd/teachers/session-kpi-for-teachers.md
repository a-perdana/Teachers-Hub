# Facilitator Session Guide — Understanding Your KPI (for Teachers)

> **Read `_facilitator-conventions.md` first** — especially §1 (ESL / EAL) and §2 (the five timing blocks).

---

## Why this session exists

Teachers often see KPI as 15 numbers to chase. They do not always know *why* a number is there, why one weighs more than another, or what they can actually *do* to move it. When KPI feels like a number-chase, teachers either ignore it or feel stressed by it.

This session changes that. Teachers learn to read the weights as their school's **priorities**, and to turn each KPI into one **concrete action** they can plan for the year. KPI becomes a plan, not a worry.

---

## Learning objectives

By the end of this session, each teacher can:

1. **Explain** what a KPI is (aspect, indicator, target, weight).
2. **Read** the weights as their school's priority order for the year.
3. **Turn** one KPI into one realistic action they will take.
4. **Say** how KPI is different from Appraisal and Competency.

---

- The one-pager `onepager-how-kpi-works.md` — one per teacher.

## Facilitator talking points

**On what a KPI is.** *"A KPI — Key Performance Indicator — is one thing your school measures this year. Each one has a number to reach and a weight. The weights add up to 100%."* (`teacher-kpi-v1.json → totalWeight = 100`).

**On weights as priorities (the key idea).** *"Look at the weights. Academic Performance, Engagement, Progress Monitoring, Classroom Management, Inclusive Teaching — these are 10% each. They are your school's top five. The 5% KPIs still matter, but the weight tells you where to put your energy first."* (`teacher-kpi-v1.json → kpis[].weight`).

**On two checkpoints.** *"Each KPI has a mid-year target and an end-of-year target. The mid-year point is not a grade. It is an early signal — so if something is off track, you have time to fix it."*

**On the Cambridge link.** *"Every KPI links to a Cambridge Teacher Standard. Progress Monitoring, for example, links to CTS 3.7 — giving feedback that moves learning forward. So your targets sit on the same standards as your appraisal and your competency growth."* (`teacher-kpi-v1.json → tk_progress_monitoring.cambridge_standard_refs`).

**On the boundary.** *"KPI measures your results this year. It does not look closely at how you teach — that is appraisal. And it is not your long-term growth journey — that is competency. Three systems, three jobs."*

## Activity instructions

### Turn one KPI into one action (22 min)
Each teacher picks **one** KPI that matters most for their class this year. They write:
- **The KPI** (e.g. "Classroom Engagement").
- **One realistic action** they will take (e.g. "use a 3-minute mini-whiteboard check every lesson").
- **By when** (a real date).

The rule: the action must be **small, dated, and in your control**. "Make all students get 90%" is not in your control. "Run a weekly retrieval quiz" is.

- **Online:** each teacher fills a shared Google Form; a few read theirs aloud.

> **Why this works:** a KPI you cannot act on creates stress. A KPI turned into one small action creates a plan. Teachers leave with a plan, not a number.

## Common questions (answer in simple English)

**"Who decides the weights and targets?"**
Your school. The 15 KPIs are network-wide, but your school sets how much each one counts and the target numbers, based on its priorities this year.

**"What if I cannot reach a target?"**
Talk to your leader at the mid-year check. The mid-year point exists so you can adjust early. Missing a target is a reason to get support, not a punishment.

**"Is a low KPI the same as a low appraisal grade?"**
No. They are separate. KPI is your results; appraisal is how you teach. One does not change the other.

**"There are 15 — that feels like a lot."**
You do not act on all 15 equally. Read the weights. Put your energy into the high-weight ones first, and pick a few small actions. You cannot do everything at once, and you are not meant to.

## Exit ticket

1. One KPI I will focus on this year, and why.
2. One action I will take, by when.
3. One thing I am still unsure about.

> Collect the action slips. They are the teacher's year plan and your evidence that the session produced something concrete.
