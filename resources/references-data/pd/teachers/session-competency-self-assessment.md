# Facilitator Session Guide — Your Competency Self-Assessment (Guided First Use)

> **Read `_facilitator-conventions.md` first** — especially §1 (ESL / EAL) and §2 (the five timing blocks).

---

## Why this session exists

The Competency Framework is live in the platform (the **Learning Path** and **My Portfolio** pages). But a teacher opening it for the first time often does not know: *Which level am I? What counts as evidence? Where do I start?* Without guidance, they either pick "Advanced" for everything (too kind to themselves) or "Awareness" for everything (too hard on themselves) — and learn nothing.

This session is a **guided first use**. Teachers do their first self-assessment with a facilitator in the room, learn what honest evidence looks like, and leave having chosen **one** competency to grow. After this session, they can use the platform alone.

---

## What the framework is (facilitator background)

The teachers track has **7 domains** and **28 competencies** (`competency_framework/teachers`, v3):

| Domain | What it covers |
|---|---|
| **SMC** — Subject Mastery & Curriculum Design | Cambridge syllabus knowledge + planning |
| **LCP** — Learner-Centred Pedagogy | responsive teaching, including EAL learners |
| **AFL** — Assessment for Learning | formative + summative evidence to guide next steps |
| **ICP** — Innovative Classroom Practice | evidence-based teaching innovation |
| **PIE** — Professional Identity & Ethics | conduct, reflection, safeguarding, CPD |
| **CCE** — Collaboration & Community Engagement | colleagues, families, the Cambridge community |
| **AID** — AI & Digital Literacy | responsible AI use + teaching students to use AI critically |

Each competency has a **4-level ladder**: **Awareness → Practitioner → Advanced → Lead**. A teacher does not get a mark. They *earn* a level by showing evidence. (Note: **EAL** = English as an Additional Language; **CPD** = Continuing Professional Development; **AI** = Artificial Intelligence; **AFL** = Assessment for Learning.)

---

## Learning objectives

By the end of this session, each teacher can:

1. **Find** their way around the Learning Path page.
2. **Pick** an honest level for one competency, using evidence — not a guess.
3. **Name** what evidence proves a level (and what does not).
4. **Choose** one competency to grow and one first step.

## Materials & setup

- The workbook `workbook-competency-self-assessment.md` — one per teacher.
- The one-pager `../00-program-overview/three-rating-systems-at-a-glance.md`.
- **A device per teacher** (laptop / tablet) logged in to their hub, OR a projected walkthrough if devices are limited.

**Face-to-face:** if devices are limited, project the Learning Path page and do the first competency together, then let pairs share a device.
**Online:** everyone is already on a device. Share your screen for the walkthrough, then send people to do their own in breakout rooms.

> **Before the session:** confirm everyone can log in. Login problems waste the most time. Have a back-up: the workbook can be done on paper if the platform is down.

## Facilitator talking points

**On "not a test".** *"This is the one rating system where you assess yourself. No one gives you a mark. You build a map of where you are, so you can choose where to grow. Being honest helps you. Being too kind to yourself only hides what to work on."*

**On the ladder.** *"Four levels: Awareness, Practitioner, Advanced, Lead. Awareness means 'I understand it.' Practitioner means 'I do it regularly in my classroom.' Advanced means 'I do it well and adapt it.' Lead means 'I help others do it.'"* (`competency_framework/teachers` → `levelOrder: ['awareness','practitioner','advanced','lead']`).

**On evidence (the heart of the session).** *"The level is not how you feel. It is what you can show. 'Advanced' is not 'I could do it.' It is 'here is the lesson, the data, the student work that shows I did.' If you cannot name the evidence, you are not at that level yet — and that is fine. That is your next step."*

**On the Cambridge link.** *"Each competency points back to a Cambridge Teacher Standard. So your growth map and your appraisal use the same picture of good teaching — they just look at it over different time periods."* (e.g. AFL competencies anchor to CTS 3.6–3.7, 4.4 — `cambridge/teacher-standards-2023.json`).

**On the AI domain.** *"There is a new domain — AI & Digital Literacy. It is here because using AI well is now part of professional teaching: use it as a tool, hold the Cambridge coursework line, and protect student data. You do not need to be an expert — Awareness is a real, honest starting level."* (`competency_framework/teachers` → domain `aid`; anchored to Cambridge GenAI Coursework Policy + UU PDP 27/2022).

## Activity instructions

### Self-assess three competencies (24 min)
Each teacher picks **3** competencies they care about (from any domain). For each:
1. Read the level descriptors on the Learning Path page.
2. Pick the level they are at **now** — honestly.
3. Write **one piece of real evidence** for that level ("I run exit tickets every lesson and adjust the next lesson — Practitioner in AFL-1").

The rule: **no level without evidence.** If they cannot name evidence, they drop one level.

- **Online:** breakout rooms of 3; each does their own on screen; the facilitator drops in to check the "evidence" habit.

### Choose one to grow (12 min)
From the three, each teacher picks **one** competency to move up one level. They write one **first step** they will take in the next two weeks.

> **Why this works:** the value is not the self-assessment number. It is the habit of tying a level to evidence, and choosing one realistic growth step. They leave with a map and a first move.

## Common questions (answer in simple English)

**"What if I am Awareness in many competencies?"**
That is normal and honest. No one is Lead in everything. A clear, honest map is far more useful than a map full of guesses.

**"Does my competency level affect my appraisal grade?"**
No. They are separate systems. A strong appraisal is good evidence *toward* a competency level, but competency also asks for things appraisal does not — like mentoring others over time.

**"Who checks my evidence?"**
You self-assess first. A reviewer can confirm your evidence when you claim a level in your portfolio. The self-assessment is yours; the confirmation keeps it fair.

**"How often do I do this?"**
It is ongoing. You move up when you are ready and have the evidence. There is no fixed deadline — this is a journey over years.

---

## Exit ticket

1. One competency I now know my honest level in.
2. One competency I will grow, and my first step.
3. One thing I am still unsure about.

> Collect line 3 — it tells you what teachers find confusing about levels or evidence, so you can sharpen the next session.
