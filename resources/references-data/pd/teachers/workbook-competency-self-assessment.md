# Workbook — Your Competency Self-Assessment

**Your name:** ______________________   **Subject:** ______________________   **Date:** ____________

This workbook helps you do your **first competency self-assessment**. It is not a test. You are building a **map of your own growth** so you can choose where to go next.

> **The one rule for today:** pick a level only if you can write down **evidence** for it. No evidence → drop one level. Honest beats kind.

---

## Part A — The 4-level ladder

Every competency has four levels. Learn them once:

| Level | What it means | How you know |
|---|---|---|
| **Awareness** | I understand it. | I can explain it. |
| **Practitioner** | I do it regularly in my classroom. | I can show it happening in my lessons. |
| **Advanced** | I do it well and I adapt it. | I can show it working *and* show how I changed it for my learners. |
| **Lead** | I help others do it. | I can show I coached or led colleagues in it. |

> **Words used here:** **competency** = one skill area of your teaching. **Evidence** = something real you can point to (a lesson plan, student work, data, a colleague you mentored). **EAL** = English as an Additional Language. **AFL** = Assessment for Learning.

## Part B — The 7 domains (your map)

You will find your competencies inside these 7 domains on the Learning Path page:

1. **SMC — Subject Mastery & Curriculum Design** — knowing the Cambridge syllabus + planning.
2. **LCP — Learner-Centred Pedagogy** — teaching for every learner, including EAL students.
3. **AFL — Assessment for Learning** — checking learning to guide the next step.
4. **ICP — Innovative Classroom Practice** — trying evidence-based new ideas.
5. **PIE — Professional Identity & Ethics** — conduct, reflection, safeguarding, growth.
6. **CCE — Collaboration & Community Engagement** — colleagues, families, the wider community.
7. **AID — AI & Digital Literacy** — using AI responsibly + teaching students to use it well.

## Part D — The evidence check

Look at your three again. Tick the honest box:

- ☐ I picked a level because I **have done it** (I can name the evidence). ✅ Good.
- ☐ I picked a level because I **could do it** or **want to do it**. ⚠️ That is one level too high. Move it down.
- ☐ I picked a level because I **understand it well** but do not do it regularly yet. → That is **Awareness**, not Practitioner.

> Remember: "Advanced" is not "I could do it well." It is "here is the proof that I did."
