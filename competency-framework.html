Competency Levels
Awareness
Demonstrates foundational understanding of the standard; follows guidance and is building confidence. (Cambridge Teacher Standard: beginning to engage)
Practitioner
Applies the competency consistently and independently; can evidence impact on student learning. (Cambridge Teacher Standard: developing)
Advanced
Adapts practice with critical reflection; uses evidence to innovate and improve outcomes for diverse learners. (Cambridge Teacher Standard: established)
Lead / Mentor
Leads and mentors colleagues; contributes to school-wide improvement and the wider Cambridge professional community. (Cambridge Teacher Standard: exemplary)

Framework Sources & References

This framework is grounded in the following internationally recognised standards and Indonesian national regulations:

📘
Cambridge Teacher Standards 2019
University of Cambridge Local Examinations Syndicate (UCLES). Defines five attributes for Cambridge-qualified teachers: Confident, Responsible, Reflective, Innovative, and Engaged. Used to map all six domains and CPD level descriptors in this framework.
cambridge.org/elt → Cambridge Teacher Standards ↗
🇮🇩
Permendiknas No. 16 Tahun 2007 — Standar Kualifikasi Akademik dan Kompetensi Guru
Kementerian Pendidikan Nasional Republik Indonesia. Establishes the four core teacher competencies (Pedagogik, Kepribadian, Sosial, Profesional) that underpin teacher certification in Indonesia. Integrated into the Professional Identity & Ethics and Collaboration domains.
peraturan.bpk.go.id → Permendiknas No. 16/2007 ↗
🎓
Cambridge CPD Framework — Professional Development Levels
Cambridge Assessment International Education. The four CPD levels (Awareness → Practitioner → Advanced → Lead/Mentor) map to the Cambridge CPD pathway and are used as the progression structure for all competency levels in this framework.
cambridge-community.org.uk → Professional Development ↗
