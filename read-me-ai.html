This page explains the AI Framework section of your Admin menu. The single page there — AI Self Assessment — is a once-a-year honest check of how you use AI in your teaching, based on the Eduversal AI Competency Framework (AICF) v1.0.

This is a growth tool, not a test. There is no pass or fail. It helps you and your school see where to focus your AI learning next — and it never feeds your KPI or Appraisal score.

AI tools (like chatbots and image generators) are now part of teaching. The AI Framework section helps you see — honestly and privately — how confident you are using AI well and safely. It has one page, AI Self Assessment, built on the Eduversal AI Competency Framework (AICF) v1.0. If a word looks new, Section 6 — Glossary explains it.

A short promise
This is not an exam. You cannot fail it. You simply read short descriptions and pick the one that sounds most like you today. The goal is to find your next small step — not to judge you. Come back here if you get stuck.
🤖
What does it measure?
Your confidence using AI across 5 areas — like using AI tools, designing lessons with AI, teaching students to use AI safely, and the ethics of it all. Each area has 3 growth levels.
👥
Who uses it?
All teaching staff. You fill it in once a year. Your line manager (your subject leader or principal) then talks it through with you — to support your growth, not to grade you.
Why a separate framework? AI moves fast and needs its own language. The AI Framework sits beside KPI, Appraisal, and the Competency Framework — but it is its own thing. Your AI self-assessment never changes your KPI or Appraisal score.

The page shows a grid: 5 domains across, 3 levels down — 15 cells in total. For each domain, you pick the one level that fits you best today.

The 3 levels

Level 1
Foundation
You can use basic AI tools safely and know the simple rules. The framework expects all staff to reach this in their first year.
Level 2
Practitioner
You use AI well in your day-to-day teaching — for planning, feedback, and resources. Most staff reach this within two years.
Level 3
Leader
You guide others, shape policy, and model good AI practice across your department or school.

The 5 domains (areas)

Each domain is one area of AI in teaching. You will see the full names and descriptions on the page itself — here is the shape:

🔧 Using AI tools 📝 Designing learning with AI 👩‍🎓 Teaching students about AI 🛡️ Ethics & safety 🌱 Professional growth
What is required? The Foundation cells are required for everyone. The Practitioner and Leader cells are optional — fill them in if they apply. Aim for honesty, not high scores.

What it is
A once-a-year self-check of your AI competency across the 5 domains × 3 levels of the AICF v1.0 framework.
When to use it
Once per year, when your school asks. You can save as you go and submit when ready.
What you see
  • A status banner at the top (not started / submitted / validated).
  • A grid of 15 cells — pick one rating per domain.
  • Foundation cells are required; Practitioner + Leader are optional.
  • A submit button — after submit, your line manager validates.
Good to know
Rate honestly. The framework expects everyone at Foundation in year one and most at Practitioner within two years — but there is no penalty for being where you are.
