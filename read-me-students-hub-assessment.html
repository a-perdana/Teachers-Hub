This page explains the Students Hub Assessment section of your Teachers menu — what each page is for, and the order you use them in. Students Hub is the separate app where your students sit their tests. These five pages are the teacher's control room for that.
For: Subject Teachers & Subject Leaders 5 pages, one simple flow Tests written centrally by Eduversal Grade 7–8 pilot
Chapter tests check mastery of one unit. They are not the same as EASE growth tests, and never the same as Practice / gamification. Each system stays separate by design.

Students Hub is a separate website where your students log in and take tests on their own devices. You don't teach there — you set it up from here, in Teachers Hub. This section has 5 pages that together let you run a chapter test (a short test that checks if students have mastered one unit). If a word looks new, Section 6 — Glossary explains it.

A short promise
You do not need to learn all 5 pages today. They always run in the same order: approve your students → schedule a test → watch it live → read the results → grade any answers a computer couldn't. Once you know the order, each page explains itself. Come back here whenever you get stuck.
📝
What is a chapter test?
A short test on one unit of work. Eduversal Subject Specialists write them centrally so every school's test is the same. Most questions grade themselves automatically. You decide when your class sits each one.
👥
Who uses this section?
Every Subject Teacher and Subject Leader running tests for a Year 7–8 class. The Essay Grader page is for Subject Leaders (and admins) only — the rest are for both.
One important separation. Chapter tests are the network's record of mastery. They do not feed the Practice / gamification games, and Practice never changes a real test score. They are deliberately kept apart so the real result stays trustworthy.

Five pages. Here is exactly what each one is for, when to use it, and what you will see.

✅
Student Approvals
Teachers · Students Hub Assessment · /student-approvals
Open
What it is
The gate. New students who picked your class appear here as Pending. You Approve them (full access) or Reject them (back to the class picker).
When to use it
First, and any time a new student joins. Clear this list before a test day.
What you see
  • Three tabs: Pending · Active · Rejected.
  • Each student's name, email, and chosen class.
  • Approve / Reject buttons.
Good to know
Rejecting does not delete a student — they just pick again. If a student is missing, they may have picked another teacher's class.
▶️
Schedule Chapter Test
Teachers · Students Hub Assessment · /test-session-launcher
Open
What it is
Where you set a test for your class. Choose a published chapter test, a class, and a date. Every active student in that class gets a draft attempt ready for the day.
When to use it
After your roster is approved, whenever you want a class to sit a unit test.
What you see
  • A list of your scheduled sessions.
  • A "Schedule a test" form: test, class, date, optional note.
  • A live count of active students in the class.
Good to know
No active students = no attempts created. Approve first. You can only schedule tests Eduversal has marked published.
👁️
Live Test Monitor
Teachers · Students Hub Assessment · /test-monitor
Open
What it is
A live board for a session in progress. It refreshes itself as students work, so you can see at a glance who needs help.
When to use it
During the test, on your own screen.
What you see
  • Per-student status: not started · in progress · submitted · flagged.
  • Tab-switch warnings (if a student leaves the test screen).
  • Cards for total / started / submitted / average / pass rate.
Good to know
This is a live view, not a grade book. The final, sorted results land on Class Assessment after submission.
📊
Class Assessment
Teachers · Students Hub Assessment · /class-assessment
Open
What it is
A colour grid: one row per student, one column per chapter test. Colours run from Emerging (red) to Exceeding (blue) so you read the whole class at a glance.
When to use it
After tests are submitted, to plan who needs more help.
What you see
  • Filters for class and subject.
  • The mastery heatmap with a 4-band legend.
  • Summary cards: active students, tests submitted, average, intervention count.
Good to know
A red cell means "this unit needs another look", not "this student is weak". It is a teaching signal, used kindly.
✍️
Essay Grader
Teachers · Students Hub Assessment · /essay-grader · Subject Leaders only
Open
What it is
A review queue for short answers that the computer couldn't match exactly (a synonym, a paraphrase, a different spelling). You award full / partial / no credit.
When to use it
After a test, when some answers are flagged for a human. As a Subject Leader (or admin).
What you see
  • Each flagged answer next to the expected answer.
  • Buttons to award credit.
  • The attempt's score updates as soon as you decide.
Good to know
An empty list is good news — every answer auto-matched and nothing needs your judgement.

A soft list, not a test. These keep your test day stress-free.

1
Approve your class the day before
Open Student Approvals early. A pending student can't sit the test, so clear the list before the bell.
2
Schedule the right test for the right class
In Schedule Chapter Test, double-check the test name and class before you launch. The student count tells you the attempts will be created.
3
Keep the monitor open during the test
The Live Test Monitor shows who is stuck so you can walk over and help. Tab-switch warnings are gentle nudges, not accusations.
4
Read the heatmap as a class, not a ranking
On Class Assessment, look for whole columns of red — that's a unit to reteach, not a list of weak children.
5
Clear the Essay Grader queue promptly
If you're a Subject Leader, finish Essay Grader soon after the test so every student's final score is fair and complete.
Tip: Practice runs and daily challenges in Students Hub are for fun and motivation — they never touch these real chapter test results. Keep them separate in your head, just like the system does.

Short, plain meanings for the terms that show up in these pages.

Students Hubthe student app
A separate website where students log in and take their tests. You set tests up from Teachers Hub; students do them on Students Hub.
Chapter testunit mastery check
A short test on one unit of work, written centrally by Eduversal. It records how well a student has mastered that unit.
Approvelet a student in
Accept a student who picked your class. Until approved, they cannot take any test.
Sessiona scheduled test
One chapter test set for one class on one date. Scheduling a session creates a draft attempt for each student.
Attemptone student's go
A single student's run at a test. It moves from draft → in progress → submitted → scored.
Flaggedneeds a human
A short answer the computer couldn't match automatically. It waits in the Essay Grader for a Subject Leader to award credit.
Mastery bandEmerging → Exceeding
The colour level on Class Assessment: Emerging (<40%), Developing (40–60%), Secure (60–80%), Exceeding (>80%). It describes the unit result.
Publishedready to schedule
A chapter test Eduversal has finished and released. Only published tests appear in the schedule list.
