Section 1

Why this page exists

Teachers Hub has many pages. On your first day, this can feel like a lot. We made this page to help you get comfortable, step by step. We will keep the language simple. If a word looks new, you will find it in Section 7 — Glossary.

A short promise
You do not need to memorise anything today. Pick the part you need. Click the link. Come back here if you get stuck. The big-picture work (pacing, appraisal, competency growth) takes months — not minutes — so there is no rush.
🏫
What is Teachers Hub?
The day-to-day portal for teachers at our partner schools. You use it to track your pacing, complete your weekly checklist, do your KPI self-assessment, write your appraisal, and grow your own teaching skills.
🎯
What is "My Work"?
A dropdown in the navbar that holds your personal pages: KPI, Appraisal, Competency, Induction. This guide explains the 5 pages you will use most often as a new teacher.

Section 3

Each personal page, explained simply

Each card below tells you four things: what the page is, why we built it, what you will see on day 1, and what to do.

📈
Learning Path
/learning-path
Open page →
What it is
A map of 24 teaching skills we want every teacher to grow. The 24 skills are grouped into 6 areas (we call them domains): Subject knowledge, Lesson craft, Assessment for learning, Inclusive classroom, Pedagogical innovation, Career growth.
Why we built it
So you know — clearly and in one place — what good teaching looks like. The 24 skills come from the Cambridge Teacher Standards (2023). Each skill has a short lesson and a practice task you can try in class this month.
Day 1 view
You will see the full list. No green ticks yet — that is normal. Click any skill to expand it. Click any of the 4 stage chips (Awareness, Practitioner, Advanced, Lead) to read the short lesson + practice activity for that stage.
What to do
Pick one skill that feels close to your current teaching. Read its Awareness lesson. That is enough for day 1.
📁
My Portfolio
/my-portfolio
Open page →
What it is
The page where you submit proof of a teaching skill at a stage. Pick the skill, pick the stage, describe what you did in class, and (optionally) attach a file.
Why we built it
Growth is not a tick-box. Showing real work — a lesson plan, a piece of student work, a short reflection — is the only honest way to claim a stage. A reviewer reads your submission and decides if the stage is granted.
Day 1 view
Empty submission form at the top and an empty "My Submissions" list below. Both are normal for a new teacher.
What to do
Do not submit on day 1. First, do the work in your classroom. Then come back here, pick the skill, write 4-6 honest sentences about what you tried, and attach a file if it helps.
  • The description must be at least 80 characters (about 2 sentences).
  • Files up to 25 MB — PDF, Word, image, or a short video.
  • After you submit, the status shows Pending. Your HQ reviewer reads it.
🏆
My Certificates
/my-certificates
Open page →
What it is
A dashboard of what you have earned. You see your certificates and a progress bar for each of the 6 teaching domains.
Why we built it
So you can see your growth in one glance. When every skill in a domain reaches the top stage (Lead), the HQ admin issues your domain certificate.
Day 1 view
All progress bars are empty. There is a friendly empty state with a button back to My Portfolio.
What to do
Nothing on day 1 — there is nothing here yet. Come back after a reviewer has approved one or two skills.
🌱
My Induction
/my-induction
Open page →
What it is
A guide for first-year teachers. It splits the year into 4 phases (Survival → Foundation → Mastery-Building → Integration). Each phase has tasks for today, this week, plus a mentor (your Subject Leader) and a private journal.
Why we built it
The first year of teaching is the hardest. Without a structure and a mentor, new teachers can burn out quickly. This page gives you small, achievable steps + a person to talk to.
Day 1 view
Two possible cases:
  • If you are a first-year teacher — you will see your mentor's name, the 4 phases, and your first tasks.
  • If you are not a year-1 teacher — you will see "You don't have an active induction". That is correct.
What to do
If you have an active induction, read the welcome at the top, meet your mentor, and look at the Today list. Your journal is private — only you and your mentor read it. HQ never reads named entries.
👥
My Mentees
/my-mentees
Open page →
What it is
A dashboard for Subject Leaders who are also mentors. It shows each first-year teacher you mentor, their pulse mood, and your cycle of monthly observations.
Why we built it
Mentoring is a separate skill from teaching. Without one place to track each mentee, important signals (a low pulse mood, a missed observation) get lost.
Day 1 view
Two cases:
  • If you are a certified mentor — you will see your mentees as cards.
  • If you are not yet certified — you will see a banner asking you to complete Mentor Certification first. This is required by Charter Non-Negotiable NN3 — only certified mentors can be assigned.
What to do
If you are a Subject Leader, open the Mentor Certification page and read through the curriculum (8 modules). Certification is issued by HQ after you finish.
Section 4

Your first week — a simple checklist

This is a soft list, not a test. Tick them off in your head — or your own notes — over the first 5 working days.

1
Sign in and check your profile
Click your avatar (top right). Make sure your name, school, subjects, and classes look correct. If something is wrong, contact tech@eduversal.org. You cannot edit role or school yourself.
2
Read this orientation page once, slowly
Sections 1, 2, and 7 are the most important. You do not need to remember every word — just know where things are.
3
Open your Weekly Checklist
Your role-specific weekly tasks. This is the page you will visit most often as a new teacher.
4
Browse your Competency Framework
Just glance. Read the 6 domain names. No need to dive deep yet — the framework will grow into your work over the year.
5
Find your pacing page
In the Pacing dropdown, open the pacing page for one of your subjects (e.g. Checkpoint Math or IGCSE Biology). This is where you track which chapters you have taught. Bookmark it.
Section 5

Notes for your role

Teachers Hub adapts to who you are. The boxes below show only the ones that apply to your role today. They may change later if a central admin updates your sub-role.

For: Teachers User (no sub-role yet)
You can browse, but most pages need a sub-role
Right now you have the basic teachers_user role with no sub-role assigned. Most Teachers Hub pages need at least one sub-role to be useful.
  • Sub-roles are: Subject Teacher, Subject Leader, Interviewer, Hiring Manager.
  • Your profile may already have one — check the profile dropdown (top right).
  • If a page redirects you to the dashboard with ?denied=... in the address, that is a sub-role gate. It is not a bug.
For: Subject Teacher
You teach in the classroom
As a Subject Teacher, your daily pages are:
  • Pacing pages for your subjects (in the Pacing dropdown) — track which chapters you have covered.
  • Weekly Checklist — small tasks every week.
  • KPI Self-Assessment and Self-Appraisal — these run on a schedule. You fill them in when HQ opens the window.
  • My Induction if you are a year-1 teacher.
For: Subject Leader
You lead a department
As a Subject Leader, you do everything a Subject Teacher does, plus:
  • Trackers — class cohort dashboards for your subject.
  • My Mentees — if you are also a certified mentor.
  • Extra weekly-checklist tab specific to Subject Leader work.
For: Mentor (Subject Leader + certified)
You guide year-1 teachers
You hold a Mentor Certification. Your responsibility is to support new teachers at your school through year 1.
  • Open My Mentees for the cohort overview.
  • Run 10 mentor observations across the year (3 of them are formal). Forms live at Observation Entry.
  • Charter NN1: induction observations never feed appraisal scoring. They are for growth, not judgement.
  • Charter NN2: HQ never reads a mentee's named journal entries. Trust matters.
For: Interviewer / Hiring Manager
You help us hire the next great teachers
Two careers-related sub-roles:
  • Interviewer — receives an emailed link to a single application's interview scorecard. Submitted scorecards are immutable.
  • Hiring Manager — opens the full Hiring Funnel for your school. Manages job positions, applications, decisions.
Both appear as a "Careers" dropdown in the navbar.
For: Teachers Admin
You manage your school's teachers
You bypass every gate in Teachers Hub. You also manage the dashboard categories on the home page. For network-wide changes (sub-role assignments, school directory), the actions still happen in Central Hub /console.

Section 7

Glossary — words you will see

Short, plain meanings for the terms that show up most often in Teachers Hub.

Competencyalso: skill
A specific teaching skill we expect you to grow. Example: "Use questioning to check student understanding". Each competency has 4 stages.
Domainalso: area
A group of related competencies. There are 6 in the Teachers track. A certificate is given per domain.
Level / Stage4 steps of growth
Awareness — you know it exists. Practitioner — you can do it with help. Advanced — you do it well on your own. Lead — you can teach or mentor others to do it.
Evidencein My Portfolio
A short written description (+ optional file) that proves you have practised a competency at a stage. A reviewer reads it before granting the stage.
CTSCambridge Teacher Standards 2023
The Cambridge document our teaching framework anchors to. Example: CTS 4.1 means standard 4.1 from that document. Click the chip to read the source text.
PermendiknasIndonesian regulation
Indonesian Ministry of Education regulations (e.g. No.16/2007 for teacher standards, No.27/2010 for induction). Cited alongside Cambridge standards where both apply.
Pacingsubject coverage
Tracking which chapters of your syllabus you have taught, when, and how much variance there is from the plan. One page per subject + year group (e.g. IGCSE Biology Year 9-10).
KPIKey Performance Indicator
A small set of measurable goals your school sets for the year. You self-assess once per period (window opens / closes from HQ).
Appraisalannual evaluation
A yearly leadership review of your teaching. Your school principal writes it, often after walkthroughs. Your self-appraisal comes first; the official version comes from your school leader.
Mentorin My Induction
A certified Subject Leader who guides a first-year teacher through year 1. Only mentors with an active Mentor Certification can be assigned (Charter Non-Negotiable NN3).
Sub-roleon your profile
An extra label beyond your basic role. TH sub-roles are subject_teacher, subject_leader, interviewer, hiring_manager. They are composable — many leaders also teach.
Chapter Testnetwork-uniform unit test
A short test authored by HQ Subject Specialists for one Cambridge unit. You launch it for a class via Test Session Launcher and watch results land in Test Monitor.
